Quick reference: common AI tools

Whether you can upload a copy often comes down to whether the tool requires you to licence ("sublicense") your input. Always check the tool's current terms — they change.

Microsoft Copilot — Enterprise (CSU staff/student login)
No sublicensingDoesn't require you to sublicense uploads and isn't used to train the model. Files are stored for 30 days then deleted.
Microsoft Copilot — Free/consumer version
Sublicensing requiredYou grant Microsoft a licence to your content, including the right to pass it on to suppliers.
ChatGPT (OpenAI)
ConditionalYou keep ownership of what you upload, but you confirm you have the rights to it. Your content may be used to improve the service unless you opt out in settings.
Claude (Anthropic)
ConditionalYou keep your rights to inputs. Content may be used for training unless you opt out in account settings (feedback or flagged content can still be used even after opting out).
Google Gemini
Sublicensing requiredYou licence Google to use uploaded content to provide and improve its services. Training is on by default but can be switched off in Gemini Apps Activity settings.
Google NotebookLM
Sublicensing requiredCovered by Google's general Terms of Service — you licence Google to use content to provide and develop the service.
Perplexity AI
Sublicensing requiredYou grant Perplexity a licence to use your content, including to publish output based on it.

For the full picture — including outputs, AI-generated content and market-share considerations — see the CSU Library Generative AI guide: Copyright.

This tool is a quick-reference aid based on CSU Library's Generative AI guide. It doesn't cover every situation and isn't legal advice — terms of use and licence agreements change, so always check the current source.
